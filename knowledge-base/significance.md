# The Significance of Kalady
Source: Official Website – Significance Page

Kalady's significance spans seven dimensions:

1. **Birthplace** — where Sri Adi Shankaracharya's journey began, before he travelled across Bharata,
   wrote his commentaries on the Upanishads, and built his enduring legacy.
2. **Sacred Landscape** — the Poorna River is central to the traditional memory of his early life; a 2017
   Sringeri Peetham discourse compared Kalady on the Poorna to Kashi on the Ganga.
3. **Aryamba (Mother)** — Kalady preserves the memory of Shankara's bond with his mother, Aryamba,
   including his departure, his promise to her, and his return. The 33rd Jagadguru of Sringeri visited the
   ashoka tree linked to her cremation before the 1910 consecration of the Kalady shrines.
4. **The Departure** — Kalady is the start of the journey: birth → Sannyasa (after the crocodile ghat
   event) → meeting Guru Govinda Bhagavatpada on the Narmada → writing the Prasthana Traya commentaries →
   establishing the Amnaya Peethams.
5. **Philosophical Significance** — Shankara's legacy is inseparable from Advaita Vedanta (non-duality);
   Kalady holds special importance for those pursuing this knowledge tradition.
6. **Rediscovery** — the birthplace had receded from public recognition over centuries. It was searched for
   by the 33rd Jagadguru of Sringeri (with support from the Maharaja of Travancore), identified through
   historical/geographical evidence, and the shrines were consecrated on **21 February 1910**.
7. **Living Tradition** — not just a monument to the past. A Veda Vedanta Pathashala was established in
   1927; the Peetham also holds a five-day Shastra conference at the Janmabhoomi during Shankara Jayanti.
