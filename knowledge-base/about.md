# About Sri Adi Shankara Janmabhoomi Kshethram
Source: Official Website – Home Page

Kalady is the birthplace (Janmabhoomi) of Jagadguru Sri Adi Shankaracharya, revered exponent of Advaita
Vedanta. Located in Kalady, Ernakulam District, Kerala, on the banks of the Purna (Poorna/Periyar) River.
Managed by Sri Sringeri Sharada Peetham (Sringeri Math), one of the four Amnaya Peethams founded by Adi
Shankaracharya.

## Sacred Shrines (Kaippilly Illam)
- **Ancient Sri Krishna Temple** — ancestral deity of Kaippilly Illam; Shankara's boyhood place of daily prayer.
- **The Crocodile Ghat** — site of the "Crocodile Miracle," where Shankara took Sanyasa (renunciation).
- **Kalady Veda Pathashala** — centre preserving oral Vedic tradition through disciplined learning.

Daily rituals at the Sri Sharadamba and Sri Adi Shankara shrines follow traditional Vedic rules.

## Deities (Source: Sringeri.net – Kalady Branch Page)

- Adi Shankara
- Samadhi of Aryamba (holy mother of Sri Shankara)

## The Kanakadhara Stotram
As a young brahmachari seeking alms, Shankara received a single withered gooseberry from an impoverished
but devoted woman — all she had. Moved by her selflessness, he composed 21 verses in praise of Goddess
Lakshmi (the Kanakadhara Stotram). Lakshmi appeared and showered golden gooseberries into the household's
courtyard, relieving their poverty — Shankara's argument being that her present selfless act outweighed
past karma.
