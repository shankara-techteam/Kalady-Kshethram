# The Life of Sri Adi Shankaracharya
Source: Official Website – Spiritual Journey / Life Page

- **Birth (c. 788 CE):** Born to Namboothiri couple Shivaguru and Aryamba in Kalady, on the banks of the
  Purna river — regarded as fulfilling Lord Shiva's promise to revive Sanatana Dharma.
- **Crocodile Miracle:** A crocodile seized young Shankara's leg while bathing in the Purna. He obtained
  his mother Aryamba's permission to take Sannyasa (renunciation) in that moment, sparing his life, marking
  the start of his monastic path.
- **The Guru's Recognition:** At the Narmada, Shankara met Sage Govinda Bhagavatpada. Asked "Who are you?",
  he replied with the Dashashloki (Nirvana Shatakam), demonstrating his realized state; the Sage recognized
  him as an avatar of Shiva and initiated him.
- **Varanasi (Kashi):** At Kedar Ghat on the Ganges, Shankara composed his Bhashyas (commentaries) on the
  Prasthana Traya (Upanishads, Brahma Sutras, Bhagavad Gita). Here he is said to have encountered Shiva
  disguised as a Chandala, inspiring the Manisha Panchakam. The Sringeri Shankar Math at Kedar Ghat
  commemorates this today.
- **Four Amnaya Peethams:** Established to safeguard the tradition, one per cardinal direction, each
  guarding one Veda: **Sringeri** (South), **Dwarka** (West), **Puri** (East), **Jyotirmath** (North).

## Key Works (Literary Legacy)
- **Bhaja Govindam** (Moha Mudgara) — urges devotion and contemplation over futile intellectual debate.
- **Vivekachudamani** — "Crest-Jewel of Discrimination"; path of discerning the Real (Atman) from the
  Unreal (Maya).
- **Soundarya Lahari** — 100 verses praising the Divine Mother Tripurasundari; key Sri Vidya tantra text.
- **Upadeshasahasri** — "A Thousand Teachings"; his only independent treatise, teaching non-duality of Self.

Core teaching: *"Brahman is the absolute reality, the world is an appearance, and the individual soul is
none other than Brahman"* (Brahma Satyam Jagan Mithya).

Sringeri Sharada Peetham, the first of the four Amnaya Peethams, has preserved the unbroken Advaita
lineage for over twelve centuries.
